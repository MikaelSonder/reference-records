Executive Summary

Transfer pricing in Austria concerns the pricing of transactions between related parties and the allocation of profits in domestic and cross-border group arrangements. Austria applies the arm’s length principle through general income tax rules and interprets transfer pricing in line with the OECD Transfer Pricing Guidelines and Austrian administrative guidance.

In operational terms, Austria combines OECD-aligned substantive analysis with a formal three-tier documentation structure under the Austrian Transfer Pricing Documentation Act, commonly known as the VPDG. This includes master file, local file and country-by-country reporting rules for qualifying multinational groups.

Austrian entities in a multinational group generally fall within the formal master file and local file documentation obligation if their turnover exceeded EUR 50 million in each of the two preceding fiscal years. Country-by-country reporting applies where consolidated group revenue reaches at least EUR 750 million.

Documentation must be prepared by the statutory deadline for the corporate income tax return and is generally submitted within 30 days upon request by the tax authority. Austria also offers an advance ruling procedure for transfer pricing matters and participates in bilateral APA practice, making the system especially relevant for multinational groups with recurring intercompany transactions, financing, holding structures or regional headquarters functions.

Decision Tree

The decision tree simplifies threshold and scope questions that commonly determine the correct Austrian transfer pricing approach.

  1. Identify whether the transaction is controlled and has Austrian tax relevance.
  2. Determine whether the Austrian entity belongs to a multinational group.
  3. Assess whether the entity exceeded EUR 50 million turnover in each of the two preceding fiscal years and therefore falls within the formal VPDG master file and local file regime.
  4. Determine whether the group reaches the EUR 750 million consolidated revenue threshold for CbCR.
  5. Ensure documentation is prepared by the statutory corporate income tax return deadline.
  6. Confirm that documentation can be submitted within 30 days if requested by the tax authority.
  7. Consider an advance ruling or bilateral APA route if the transaction is recurring, material or likely to create significant cross-border controversy risk.
