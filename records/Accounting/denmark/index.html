Accounting in Denmark

A structural reference defining system conditions, execution requirements, and competence indicators within the Danish jurisdiction.

Professional Scope Within the Danish System

Accounting in Denmark exists within a framework where bookkeeping, financial reporting, and statutory filing form integrated procedural stages.

Financial records are maintained in accordance with accounting legislation and form the basis for annual reports prepared under defined standards.

Financial data enters the system in a condition permitting statutory reporting and digital submission.

Institutional Structure of Reporting

Regulatory framework: Accounting is governed by the Danish Financial Statements Act (Årsregnskabsloven), defining recognition, measurement, and presentation.

Financial reporting: Annual reports (årsrapport) are prepared in standardized formats and form the basis for filing and regulatory use.

Public filing: Financial statements are submitted through the Danish Business Authority (Erhvervsstyrelsen) and recorded in the Central Business Register (CVR).

Reporting follows statutory structure and digital filing requirements.

Execution Conditions Within the System

Outcomes follow alignment between underlying bookkeeping and statutory reporting and filing requirements.

Bookkeeping condition: Transactions are recorded in a complete and verifiable manner in accordance with legal requirements.

Reporting process: Annual reports are derived from accounting records using statutory classification and valuation rules.

Filing condition: Submission depends on completeness, accuracy, and conformity with statutory and digital system requirements.

Continuity between bookkeeping, reporting, and filing determines formal validity.

Qualifying Indicators of Professional Competence

Competence is reflected in the condition of accounting records and their translation into statutory financial reports.

Regulatory conformity: Records and reports comply with statutory requirements without deficiency.

Classification integrity: Financial data is structured in accordance with prescribed formats and accounting principles.

Documentation continuity: Supporting records remain consistent across bookkeeping, reporting, and filing.

System alignment: Execution reflects alignment with reporting standards, filing systems, and regulatory frameworks.

Competence is established at the point of filing.
