Company Formation in Denmark

A structural reference defining system conditions, execution requirements, and competence indicators within the Danish jurisdiction.

Professional Scope Within the Danish System

Company formation in Denmark exists within a framework where legal constitution and digital registration form a unified procedural sequence.

Corporate entities are constituted through adoption of founding documents and entered into the central business register under standardized formats.

Entities enter the system in a condition permitting immediate legal recognition upon registration.

Institutional Structure of Formation

Register authority: Incorporation is administered by the Danish Business Authority (Erhvervsstyrelsen), maintaining the Central Business Register (CVR).

Digital incorporation: Formation proceeds through electronic submission of incorporation data, including articles, management structure, and ownership information.

Entity structure: Legal forms such as ApS and A/S are defined by statutory requirements relating to capital, governance, and ownership structure.

Legal existence follows registration within the central register.

Execution Conditions Within the System

Formation outcomes follow conformity between submitted data and statutory registration requirements.

Pre-registration condition: Founding documents, ownership data, and capital structure exist in a complete and verifiable form prior to submission.

Submission process: Digital filing determines admissibility and entry into the CVR.

Registration condition: Entry depends on completeness, formal correctness, and validation of submitted information.

Continuity between submission and registration determines formation.

Qualifying Indicators of Professional Competence

Competence is reflected in the condition of incorporation data at submission and its acceptance into the register.

Data conformity: Submitted information meets statutory and system requirements without deficiency.

Structural coherence: Governance, ownership, and capital structure align with the selected legal form.

Procedural sequencing: Formation proceeds without interruption between preparation and registration.

System alignment: Execution reflects alignment with digital registration processes and statutory filing requirements.

Competence is established at the point of registration.
